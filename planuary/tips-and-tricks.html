First: Curate your art style for speed. If you want to make progress on your comic fast, designing the style it is rendered in to be produced quickly and comfortably is key. If you can make pages fast, you'll be able to see your body of work build up fastâ€” and nothing is more satisfying than printing off a tall stack of pages you drew yourself.

Second: If you have a plot in mind, write out as much of the story ahead of page production as possible. If you want to create a gag-a-day style comic, script a bunch of jokes ahead of page production as well. This way, you'll be able to know what direction you want to take the comic in if it's a narrative comic and have something to fall back on if you run out of ideas for your gag-a-day comic.

Finally: Keep your favorite comics close at hand as you develop your idea. What do you like about them? Try to dissect them and figure out what makes them tick. Do you like the character designs of one, and the humor of the other? What about the backgrounds of one and the dialogue of another? See if you can mash all those traits together under the umbrella of your favorite comics genre to make your comic as fun as possible.

Webtoon is a popular distributor of webcomics that features a scrolling format. Check it out for inspiration and see if you like the style of comic available! Who knows--- you might want to host your comic here!

Tapas is another common distributor of webcomics. It allows you to post comics in scrolling or page format. There's a lot of fun comics here--- explore the library of both paid and free comics for inspiration!

24 Hour Comics Day is a challenge where you create a 24-page comic book story within 24 hours. It's traditionally hosted on October 7th, but you can do it on another day for an informal challenge.

Procreate is an iOS app well-loved by comic creators all around. If you have an iPad, it's highly recommended as an art app.

Medibang is a comic creation and digital painting app that is free and loved by PC and tablet-using artists all around. Try it out if you want a free alternative to Procreate!
